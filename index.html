Memorial Bell Tower

Memorial Bell Tower, an iconic feature of the Phillips Academy campus, is a memorial to the 87 alumni who were killed in World War I. Located at the corner of Salem Street and Main Street, the 159-foot tower rings every half-hour.


Street Address: 200 Main St, Andover, MA 01810

Coordinates: 42.64596268710454, -71.1326203



















Borden Memorial Gym

Borden Memorial Gym is home to Phillips Academy's physical education deparment, basketball courts, volleyball courts, pool, and locker rooms. Andover's fitness center is also located inside the building.

Bulfinch Hall

Bulfinch Hall is home to the Phillips Academy English Department. The building, constructed in 1818 and renovated in 2012, exemplifies the colonial style of architecture.

Paresky Commons

Paresky Commons serves as Phillips Academy's main dining facility. Paresky Commons was built in the 1920s in the colonial revival style. The building contains 4 dining areas and is LEED certified.

Gelb Science Center

The Gelb Science center is home to Andover's physics, chemistry, and biology departments. An state-of-the-art astronomy observatory with several telescopes is located on the building's roof.

Morse Hall

Morse Hall classrooms are used for math, statistics, and computer science courses. The building is named after Samuel Morse, the inventor of the telegraph and Phillips Academy alum.

Pearson Hall

Pearson Hall, home to the Latin and Greek departments and the Academic Skills Center, is named after Phillips Academy's first head of school Eliphalet Pearson. Pearson Hall was built in 1817.

Oliver Wendell Holmes Library

The Oliver Wendell Holmes Library was built in 1929 and boasts over 80,000 books. The building contains the historic Garver Room, which is used for silent study, and the Nest, the academy's makerspace.

Samuel Phillips Hall

Samuel Phillips Hall, named after Phillips Academy's founder, is home to Andover's foreign language and social science departments. Commencment is held anually on the steps of the building's iconic facade.
